    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1 9th year of the king Parakesarivarman. 

    2-10 We, the great people of the assembly (peruṅkuṟi perumakkaḷōm) of the Śrī-Kāmaravalli-caturvetimaṅkalam, a brahmadeya on the north [of the river]. After having the drum beaten (taṭṭaḻi koṭṭi) on the auspicious day of Kārttikai—which this year happened on a Thursday of the Dhanu month—the assembly, for the merit (cāṟṟi) of the assembly, gathered together in the maṇḍapa where we have granted an allowance (jivitam) for the maintenance of the holy offering (śrīpali) and the ritual of waking up of the Lord (tiruppaḷḷi eḻucci)—as long as the sun and the moon endure—for our god Tirunallūr Parameśvara, which has gracefully taken abode, and it consists of (āvatu):

    10-14 so as to obtain (koḷḷa-p peṟuvat’ āka) on every harvest (pūttōṟum):

    • a patakku of paddy and a tūṇi for each mā extending (virivu ) to all land that grows (viḷaiyum) rice irrigated (pūci) with water (nīr? ) below (kīḻ↓1) the holy (śrī) Kōtaṇṭarāma channel, [and]
    • a kuṟuṇi of paddy for each mā in case the paddy is attached to the embankment (aṇai ).

    14-16 so that the village commitee from the great assembly authorises people (iṭakkaṭavar) to enter the garden (tōppu) during new moon. 

    17-18 the temple administrators (tēvakaṉmikaḷ), according to [...], appoint people to collect taxes [...]

    18-20 so that the [service of] beating [of the drums] (koṭṭuvataṟku) in śrībali happens, as long as the sun and the moon endure, taking this paddy in this way (ipparicu):

    20-22 through the order of Bhaṭṭaṉ Nakkanākaṉ from Nallūr in all the four directions,

    22-24 through the order of Nārayaṇaṉ Nārayaṇaṉ from Kaṭavūr

    24-25 through the order of Tirukkuṟuṅkuṭi Kramavittaṉ from the royal mutt (kō-māṭam)

    25-27 through the order of Pattipōḻ Nārāyaṇahaṭṭaṉ from Kirāñcikkam.

    27-29 We, the great people of the assembly, have had it engraved in stone it stays like this (niṟppatu) as long as the sun and the moon endure.

    30-34 Through the order (paṇiyāl) of the village officer (madhyastaṉ) Amaṇamaṅkalayaṉ, alias ... nūṟṟuvan from the three hundred [officers]. This is my signature.

    34-36 Through the order (paṇiyāl) of the village officer (madhyastaṉ) Brahmapiriyaṉ. This is my signature.

    36-39 Through the order (paṇiyāl) [commanded by] me Pūtaṉ Śrīkaṇṭa, alias Peruṅkāviti from Marutūr.

    40-42 Through the order (paṇiyāl) of 74 village officers (madhyastaṉ) .

    42-44 Through the order (paṇiyāl) of the village officer (madhyastaṉ) Tiruveḷḷarai Peruṅkāviti[...].
