    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1 Prosperity! Fortune!

    6-7 27th year of the glorious Lord Rājādhirāja, alias king Rājakesarivarman

    1-6 who, ... [meykkīrtti of king Rājādhirāja I]

    7-10 To whomever kingsmen of K ... We have given, according to the royal command (tirumukap paṭi), which previously had him, along with [other] respected men/brahmins (aiyariṭaiyil), as the recipient (peṟṟuṭaiya) [of the endowment]

    • saying "We have given, as ritual (nimittya) provisions (tāṉa), for [the benefit] of all our people, to Ampalattāṭi Tirunāvukkaraiyaṉ and his lineage, who are the reciters of holy hymns (tiruppatiyam viṇṇappam ceyum) at/for the tēvāram↓1 of the monastery of the temple [dedicated to] Mahādevar in Tiruvaṭakuṭi within the Uraiyūr-kūṟṟam in Kēraḷāntaka-vaḷa-ṇāṭu [that belongs to] Kōnerimaikoṇṭāṉ" ;
    • and also saying "We commanded (coṉṉām) to take (koḷḷa) one tūṇi of paddy daily for the maintenance (pōkam-āka) of the monastery (maṭam) from the treasury of this god."

    10-11 We commanded “He, the recipient (peṟuvāṉ), shall

    • make the endowment (nivantaṅkaḷ ceyyavum) to the temple servants (nivantakkāṟar) who perform various services [in the temple], at the aforementioned rate (coṉṉa-vaṇṇamē),
    • rotate (māṟṟavum) and appoint (iṭavum) the endowments and the temple servants who perform different services, including the śaiva brahmins of this god.”
