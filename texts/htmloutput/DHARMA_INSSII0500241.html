    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1 Prosperity! Fortune! In progress...

    1-5 In the name of our army commander (ceṉāpatikaḷ), this land is given so that the hereditary rights of the land (kāṇi) of our army commander [which belonged to] the landowner (uṭaiyār) from Rājarāja Paṅkala-nāṭu, alias Kaṇṭarātittaṉ Ceṭṭi [of the court of king] Kōṉerimaikoṇṭāṉ [are transferred] to the managers of the sacred temple (cikāriyañ ceyvārkaḷ) of Tiruvāṉpaṭṭi-uṭaiyār.

    5-11 For the Paṅkuṉi festival [dedicated] to this God, he gave to Viṇṇāḷaṉ (?) three pots of paddy seeds [obtained] from the plot to the east of the Koṅkakulavalli cross-channel and north of the Atirācarāca canal in Civapāta-cēkara Maṉṉaṟai located in Nallūr. [This is a plot under the charge of] Nācci (?) and the assembly of Vīrakeraḷa Nallūr, alias Kovanputtūr, in Pērūr-nāṭu.

    11-13 [This was done] so the expenses amounting to twenty-four pots of paddy claimed by the landholder (mēlvāram) [are covered] by twenty pots of paddy seeds.

    13-14 For the maṭam, one tūṇi, one patakku↓1, and one kalam of paddy, which includes four side dishes (veñcaṉam), one tūṇi and one paṭakku of sacred rice offerings.

    14-16 For the temple priests who perform the worship, six nāḻis, one tūṇi, and 15 pots of paddy, which include a side-dish per four pots of rice.

    16-17 For the temple priests who perform the worship, for those who play the holy flute, the garland maker (toṭuppāṉ), and the plucker (paṟippāṉ) that arranges the garland of the idol, six nāḻis, one tūṇi, and 15 pots of paddy, which include a side-dish per four pots of rice.
