    Citamparam, Nāṭarāja temple, time of Rājendra Cōḻa I, year 24

    Metadata

    Current Version:  draft, 2024-03-18Z

    Editors:   Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis.

    DHARMA Identifier: INSSII0400223

    Summary: List of expenses given for the Tiruvātirai festival celebrated in Cidambaram. The inscription includes donations of gold coins ( kācu ) to Śrīmāheśvaras, reciters of Tiruttoṇṭattokai, weavers, oil sellers, merchants, among others.

    Hand Description:

    Medial i and ī interchangeable.

    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1 Prosperity! Fortune!

    15 24th year of the glorious king Rājentiracōḻa, the Lord, alias the king Irācakēcaripaṉmar,

    1-14who ... [meykkīrtti of Rājendra I Cōḻa]

    15-16 On the day of the festival of the sacred month of Aṉi [organized] for the Lord of Tirucciṟṟampalam, master of Nakkaṉ↓1 Pāvai, female attendant (aṇukki) to the king Rājentiracōḻa, [his image] shall be gracefully erected (eḻuntaruḷum).

    16 A thousand pots of cooked rice shall be distributed to the śrīmaheśvaras at the time of dinner (amutuceytaruḷum) and for the offering of rice donated (aḻikkum) to the Lord.

    16-17 Two gold coins for the expenditures including the garments (paricaṭṭam) and gold gracefully given for the oil (eṇṇey) [used during the festival] for the Lord.

    17-18 The endowment has been previously determined according to the attested copy (teṟiṉa piramāṇappaṭi) from the town Kuṇamemeṉkaipuram, [which states that] the land is taken from a section of Parākramacōḻanallūr in the Kiṭārakoṇṭacōḻa-periḷamai↓2-nāṭu, a hamlet (piṭākai) to the east of Perumpaṟṟappuliyūr, an independent town (taṉiyūr) from the current Rājentira-ciṅkaḷanāṭu.

    Commentary

    According to epigraphist, from line 7 (takkaṇalāṭamum), “the wording is quite different from that of the other published inscriptions”.
