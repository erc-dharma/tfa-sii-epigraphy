    Tiruviṭaimarutūr, Mahāliṅgasvāmin temple, Uttama Cōḻa, 7th year

    Metadata

    Current Version:  draft, 2024-04-02Z

    Editor:   Renato Dávalos.

    DHARMA Identifier: INSSII1900181

    Summary: A lacunose inscription, listing several endowments: an undated hereditary land endowment for singing (tēci pāṭu) before the Lord Tiruviṭaimarutuṭaiyār at the time assembly hall ( tiruvōlakkam ) and another land endowment dated to the seventh regnal year of Parakesarivarman to various gods in Tiruviṭaimarutūr.

    Hand Description:

    Only Fragment 1 refers to the singing as tēciy pāṭu, and only one instance we find tēciya pāṭu. Fragment 2 does not record this singing.

    Fragment 1

    1-2 [...] Maturāntaka Muvēnta Vēḷār, the superintendent (aṭikārikaḷ) who does the sacred service (śrīkāryam ceyyum) [for] the Lord of Tiruviṭaimarutu, and the manager (tēvakaṉmi) [of the temple] of the Lord of Tiruviṭaimarutu [...]

    2-3 the assembly [...] the queen, the great goddess (mābhaṭṭārakiyar) [...]

    3 For Eḻupaṇai Tēvaṉ[...] Kaśyapan Kaṇṭa, singer of folk songs (tēciy pāṭum) while sitting at the assembly hall (tiruvōlakkam) of the Lord dwelling in the shrine [...] in the temple of the Lord [of Tiruviṭaimarutu...]

    4 The vēli of land for Eḻupaṇai Tēvaṉ that was previously inscribed on stone for singing tēci [songs] performed [during the dance] of tēci.

    4-6 the accountant who oversees [the temple of] the Mahādeva [of the Mūlasthāṉam temple at] Tiruviṭaimarutūr ↓1 [...], accountant who oversees the temple of the Lord of Tiruviṭaimarutūr for the goddess (mā) Bhaṭṭaraki [...] queen of [...] assembly hall [...].

    1-2 [...] I have given this for the the Brahmans (paṭṭarkaḷukkum) of the temple which I have built in this village ...

    2-4 Seventh year of the king Parakesarivarman, day <...> thirtieth year of the king Parakesarivarman, who took Maturai, [... ...] This is the manner in which we, the members of the assembly of Vaikal, a brahmadeya in Veṇṇāṭu [...]

    4-5 We have become the donors of the obligatory supply of free food (eccōṟu kāttu)↓2 and the tax that came out of the two vēlis of land [... which was given] in the 30th year [of Parāntaka I]↓3. Two vēlis of land [...] Nakkaṉ [... the shrine called] Talaikkōli-Viṇṇakar at Tiruviṭaimarutūr, to the Mahādeva [in the Mūlasthāṉam] at Tiruviṭaimarutūr, and to Talaikkōli↓4 in Tiruviṭaimarutūr.

    Commentary

    The status of this inscription is not clear from the editions available. It appears that a single record has received by mistake two ARIE numbers. SII 19, no. 181 is the edition of ARIE/1907-1908/B/1907/234, whereas SII 23, no. 233 is the edition of ARIE/1907-1908/B/1907/233, but the 6 + 2 lines of the latter correspond to lines 1-8 of the former. Srinivasa Rao, Sircar, and Gai 1967 note ad SII 19, no. 181: "This together with No. 233 of 1907 seems to form a single inscription with some portions lost, and hence with gaps in the continuity of lines." Srinivasa Rao 1979 notes ad SII 23, no. 233: "This seems to be part of a bigger inscription, evidently No. 234, of which the beginning is lost. being also damaged the sequence is not clear after the first six lines." In Srinivasa Rao 1979, no text is published for SII 23, no. 234, and the reader is redirected to SII 19, no. 181.

    This seems to be a recapitulative inscription listing several endowments.

    All the lines in fragment 2 are evidently lacunose at their ends, even though not indicated in Srinivasa Rao, Sircar, and Gai 1967.

    Notes

    ↑1. Tiruviṭaimarutil Mahādeva is the name of the deity in the shrine, which already appears in inscriptions from an earlier date, e.g., appears in SII 5, no. 719 (dated in 931 CE) or SII 23, no. 262 (dated in 925 CE).
    ↑2. Pondicherry Inscriptions 342.4 has the version eccōṟu kūṟṟu, but probably with the same meaning of observing (kāttu) that free food (eccōṟu) is given to devotees during the time of the festival.
    ↑3. Probably refers to the endowment of two vēlis that is recorded in SII 23, no. 258. Here, the land was used (āṉa?) for a garden of vañci flowers (vañcittōṭṭam)
    ↑4. This title given to someone who stands out in music. Moreover, it appears already in an inscription dated in the year 907 (seventy years before), where a certain Taliccēri Tēvaṉār, was included in a donation of lands, from which she received nine mās of land, according to SII 23, no.233.
