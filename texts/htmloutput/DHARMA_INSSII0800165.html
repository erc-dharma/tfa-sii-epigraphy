    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1Prosperity!

    1-8 The illustrious mahārācāti-irāja-irāca-paramēśvaran, the vanquisher of the three kings (mūvarayara kaṇṭaṉ), the destroyer of the enemy kings (ariya-irāyavipāṭaṉ), the frightener of the mind of the kings of the eight direction (aṣṭatikku irāyamaṉōpayaṅkaraṉ) (the frightener of the mind of the kings of the eight direction), the vanquisher of the kings who ... (pāṣaikkut tappu varāyara kaṇṭaṉ), the lord of the oceans of the east, the south and the west (pūruva-tekṣiṇa-paccima-camuttirāticuraṉ), the preceptor who established the kingdom of the Yamunā, (yamuṉai-irācciya-sthāpaṉācāriya), the destroyer of the Kecapati↓1 (kecapativipāṭaṉ), śrī-Vīrappiratāpa śrī-Vīra Kiṣṇatēvamahārāyar having graciously appeared in Vicaiyanakaram↓2,

    9-27 having graciously appeared so that there be (āka) victory in the eastern direction, he took the fort of Utaiyakiri↓3, he captured Irā-uttarāya-mahāpattiram, he captured at one bound Attaṅki, Vinikoṇṭai, Velaṅkoṇṭai, Nākāccuṉiturkkam, Taṅkeṭak Kētavaram and other mountain forts, he captured the fort of Koṇṭavīṭu after attacking it, he captured alive the pāttira-cāmantars such as Piṟatāparuttiraṉ, Kecapatirāyaṉ, Kumāra Vīrapattirarāyaṉ, Narakaritēvaṉ, the son of Kumāra A[ha]mmīrapāttiraṉ, Mallukāṉaṉ↓4 of Rāccūr, Uttaṇṭakāṉaṉ↓5, Kacavāpāttira of Caṉṉiyālam, Pūcalappāṭi Rācirācu, Śrīnātarācu and Ilakṣmipatirācu and Paccima Vālacantira Mahāpāttiram, etc. and gave them the hand of protection, he graciously performed the tulāpuruṣa-mahādānain the Amarēcuram [temple in] Taraṇikōṭṭam, he then returned and graciously appeared in Vicaiyanakaram↓6,

    27-43 so that there be (āka) victory in the direction of the Kaliṅga country, he visited Pacavāṭai, took the fort of Koṇṭapaḷḷi, where he captured alive several pāṭṭiras such as Piṟakarāca Ciṟaccantiraṉ mahāpāttiran [] mahāpāttira, and Pijilikāṉaṉ, etc. and gave them the hand of protection, he captured Aṉantakiri, Uṇṭaṟkoṇṭa, Uruḷakkoṇṭa, Aruvappaḷḷi, Callippaḷḷi, Attikkoṇṭa, Kappaluvāyi Nallaṅkoṇṭa [] Kaṉakakiri, Caṅkarakiri and [] tiṟamalli, he visited Ciṅkāttiri, and Poṭṭuṉūr where he erected a victory pillar and from Ciṁhātti [] he returned, he graciously appeared in Irācamayēntiṟāvaram, he performed mahādāna for the sake of his queens Ciṇṇācci Ammaṉ Tirumalaitēvi Ammaṉ, and he returned to Vicaiyanakaram↓7.

    43 Prosperity! Fortune!

    43-56 In the year Tātu, in the month of Makara, in the bright fortnight, tirteenth day, a Monday, which currently corresponds with the year 1438 of the victorious and wonderful Cālivākaṉa Cakas,↓8 he graciouly visited Śrī-Kaḷasti, worshipped the god Kāḷattinātaṉ, had graciously a hundred-pillared hall (maṇṭapam) and a big entrance pavillion (kōpuram) built, ... the temple sevices that the king Kṛṣṇarāyamahārāyar had made as pious donation for the Lord who owns ... and for Uṇṇāmalai Nācciyār, are:
