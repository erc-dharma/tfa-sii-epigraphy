    Editors:   Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis.

    DHARMA Identifier: INSSII0800165

    Summary: Gift of land for performing various services during festival, including adhyayana.

    Hand Description:

    Medial i and ī interchangeable.

    Translation by Renato Dávalos and Emmanuel Francis

    1-8 Hail! the illustrious mahārācāti-irāca, irācaparamēśvaran, mūvarayara-kaṇṭa (the vanquisher of the three kings), ariirāyavipāṭa (the destroyer of the enemy kings) aṣṭatikku irāyamaṉōpayaṅkara (the frightener of the mind of the kings of the eight direction), Bāṣaikkuttappuvarāyarakaṇṭa, pūruva-tekṣiṇa-paccima-camuttirāticura (lord of the oceans of the east, the south and the west), Yamuṉai irācciyasthāpaṉācāriya (the preceptor who established the kingdom of the Yamunā), and Kecapativipāṭa (the destroyer of Kecapati), śrī-Vīrappiratāpa śrī-Vīra Kiṣṇatēvamahārāyar is ruling at Vicayanakara.

    9-10 In the course of his campaign to conquer the eastern direction, he took the fort of Utaiyakiri and captured Irāuttarāya mahāpattira;

    11-15 he capture at one bound Attaṅki, Vinikoṇṭai, Velaṅkoṇṭai, Nākāccuṉiturkkam, Taṅkeṭak Kētavaram and other mountain forts;

    15-17 he captured the fort of Koṇṭavīṭu after attacking it and

    17-25 he captured alive the pāttira cāmantās like Piṟatāparuttiraṉ Kecapatirāyaṉ’s son Kumāra-Vīrapattirarāyaṉ, Naraka[ha]tēvaṉ, the son of Kumāra-A[ha]mmīrapāttiraṉ, Mallukāṉaṉ[khān] of Rāccūr, Uttaṇṭakāṉaṉ[khān], Kacavāpāttira of Caṉṉiyāla, Pūcalappāṭi Rācirācu, Śrīnātarācu and Ilakṣmipatirācu and Paccima Vālacantira mahāpāttiram, and gave them the hand of protection. 

    25-26 Then he visited Taraṇikōṭṭam where he performed the tulāpuruṣa-mahātāṉam [gift of things equal in weight of a donor] in the temple of Amarēcura.

    27 Then he returned to Vicaiyanakara.

    27-33 Again he started for the conquest of Kaḷiṅkatēcam when he visited Pacavāṭai, took the fort of Koṇṭapaḷḷi, where he captured alive several pāṭṭiras like Piṟakarāca Ciṟaccantira mahāpāttiran [] mahāpāttira, and Pijilikāṉaṉ and gave them the hand of protection. 

    33-37 Then he captured Aṉantakiri, Uṇṭṟakoṇṭa, Uruḷakkoṇṭa, Aruvappaḷḷi, Callippaḷḷi, Attikkoṇṭa, Kappaluvāyi Nallaṅkoṇṭa [] Kaṉakakiri, Caṅkarakiri and [] tiṟamalli.

    38-40 He, then visited Ciṅkāttiri, and Poṭṭuṉūr where he erected a victory pillar and from Ciṁhātti [] he returned and visited Irācamayēntiṟāvaram.

    41-42 [There his] queens Ciṇṇācci Ammaṉ Tirumalaitēvi Ammaṉ performed mahātānam.

    42-43 [Then] he returned to Vicaiyanakaram.

    43-49 Hail! Prosperity! In the expried victorious and wonderful Cālivākaṉa Caka year 1438, in the year Tātu, on Monday the 13th day of the bright fortnight of the month of Makara↓1, [the king i.e., Kṛṣṇatēvamahārāyar] visited Śrī-Kaḷasti, worshipped the god Kāḷattināta, and provided for the god a 100-pillared maṇṭapam and the big kōpura.

    Commentary

    According to Srinivasan and Reiniche 1990, the slab to which this inscription belonged has been lost.
